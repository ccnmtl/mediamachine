Michael (Edward Furlong) a teenage video game enthusiast discovers Brainscan, a new game that is more than merely engrossing. Michael's life actually begins to merge into the world of the game.<br><br>In this clip, Michael dreams of a gruesome surgery. The protagonist in the dream cries out for his mother while silent doctors hover above him, waiting to anesthetize their patient. Michael awakes troubled and with a large scar on his own knee. What might Michael's dream signify about the experiences of adolescents in their fantasy lives? When fantasy experiences become more than mental experimentation, but are acted out in real or virtual life, what are the ramifications? This clip points toward a more sinister connection between the actual and virtual elements of adolescence. (jlh)<br>
