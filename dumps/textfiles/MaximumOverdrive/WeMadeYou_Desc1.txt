The patrons of the Dixie Boy Truck Stop are trapped and at the mercy of a circling pack of 18-wheeler trucks. As this clip begins, the camera moves from an airbrushed painting of the Last Supper to the diner's jukebox, with then explodes. The comparisons between the predicament of a culture that is being victimized by machines that it created and the last days of Christ are not subtle at all in this film. But where is the martyr who will die for our sins, the hubris of believing that we could control something we created? Apparently, it is not the waitress (Ellen McElduff). (jlh)
