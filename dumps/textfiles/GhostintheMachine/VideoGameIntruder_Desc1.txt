As Josh (Wil Horneff) and a friend play a virtual reality video game, an intruder enters their frequency. Karl (Ted Marcoux), a serial killer whose untimely death during a CAT scan somehow absorbs his body into the city's computer network, has infiltrated the shared experience of the two boys and seems to have plans for Josh.<br><br>The game involves a world in which only two players exist with the understanding that the goal is to kill or be killed. However, when a third player enters into the game, this seems to unify the boys toward figuring out the nature of the unexpected presence. Can we ever truly control our environment, even if it is one we have carefully engineered? What does the presence of the third person, indeed, a grown man in these boys' game represent? (jlh)<br>
