The 'Address Book Killer' strikes again. Karl Hochman, who during an unfortunate CAT scan accident was absorbed into his town's computer network, now carries out his murderous intentions via the power lines of the city. This evening, the death du jour is that of an unsuspecting computer hobbyist who, while microwaving his dinner, himself is done in by this cooking method that we always suspected wasn't particularly healthy.<br><br>As we watch everything in the kitchen bubble and bake, one gets the feeling that at any moment, even the most benign piece of technology could turn against us. Indeed, if a microwave oven can be an agent of death, what's to stop your Walkman or your cellular phone from being the next vehicle of gruesome torture? (jlh)<br>
